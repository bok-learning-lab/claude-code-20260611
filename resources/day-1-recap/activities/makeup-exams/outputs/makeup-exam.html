Part 3: Calculations  (30 points — 10 pts each)

Show all work. Include units throughout.

15. (10 pts) Diffusion

A dye molecule has a diffusion coefficient of D = 5 × 10−10 m2/s in water at room temperature.

(a) Using the random walk model (x = √(2Dt)), estimate how far (in micrometers) the dye molecule diffuses on average in t = 1 second.
(b) A spherification droplet has a radius of 5 mm. How long would it take for the dye to diffuse from the center to the surface? (Use the same formula, solving for t.)
(c) A chef wants the gel shell to form to a thickness of 0.5 mm in about 30 seconds. Based on your calculation, is this physically reasonable? Briefly explain.

16. (10 pts) Heat Transfer and Cooking Time

The thermal diffusivity of a potato is approximately α = 1.4 × 10−7 m2/s. A potato has a roughly spherical shape with radius R = 4 cm.

(a) Using the thermal diffusion timescale τ = R2/α, estimate the characteristic time (in minutes) for the center of the potato to reach the oven temperature.
(b) If the same calculation is repeated for a baby potato with radius R = 1.5 cm, what is the new cooking time? What does this tell you about scaling laws in cooking?
(c) A chef claims that cutting the potato in half will reduce the cooking time by exactly half. Is this correct? Explain quantitatively using the formula above.

17. (10 pts) Viscosity

At 20°C, olive oil has a dynamic viscosity of η = 84 mPa·s. Water at the same temperature has η = 1 mPa·s. Suppose that heating olive oil from 20°C to 60°C reduces its viscosity by a factor of 4.

(a) What is the viscosity of olive oil at 60°C?
(b) A chef wants to make a vinaigrette that stays emulsified after shaking. Should she use room-temperature olive oil (20°C) or warmed olive oil (60°C) to get smaller oil droplets? Why? (Hint: think about what viscosity means for droplet breakup under shear.)
(c) If the chef adds a tablespoon of Dijon mustard (which contains mucilage particles that act as emulsifiers), will her vinaigrette be more or less stable than without the mustard? Explain in terms of what happens at the oil-water interface.
