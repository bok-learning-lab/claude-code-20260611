What You Can Make with Claude faculty projects, ordered from familiar text toward unfamiliar code — and past it

A review of what faculty are building, arranged on one axis. It begins in the writing you already command and descends toward forms that did not exist two years ago. Each row is a genre of output. Examples at left. One project unpacked at right: situation, inputs, operations, outputs, and the interface suited to it (ChatCoworkCode). The loop is constant — inputs, operations, outputs. Only the medium changes. Begin where you are fluent. Descend.

Unpacked

Meeting notes → a clean report for the dean

SituationMessy notes from a faculty meeting; a structured report due tomorrow.
Inputsmeeting-notes.md — your raw bullet points.
OperationsOne prompt: "turn these into a report with a summary, decisions made, and action items with owners and dates."
Outputsreport.md, or a polished .docx on request.
Best fitChatCoworkCode if repeated monthly

4Bots & Connected Tools MCP, Slack Today's topic

Examples

  • Claude connected to Semantic Scholar for live literature search

  • A Slack bot that answers from your course materials
  • An oral-exam practice bot
  • Claude connected to your Google Drive

Unpacked

An art-history lecture, illustrated from the museum's own records

SituationPlain lecture notes name a dozen works. The illustrated page must show the real objects — fetched, not remembered.
InputsThe lecture notes; a free Harvard Art Museums API key in .mcp.json.
OperationsA custom MCP gives Claude the museum's search and object tools. It looks up each work, pulls images and tombstone metadata, builds the page.
OutputsAn illustrated lecture page where every image and caption traces to an accession record.
Best fitCodeMCP servers run here

Unpacked

Calvino's Six Memos, by the numbers

SituationStudents see literary qualities as a measurable space — and plot their own prose against it.
InputsThe five memos as text; definitions for each measure.
OperationsDeterministic text analysis + a 2D embedding map + a live draft composer, charts drawn in d3.
OutputsA live page where pasted prose appears in every chart in real time.

6Agentic Pipelines & Systems Most complex

Examples

Unpacked

Naming every writer cited across 538 Dylan songs

SituationA close reading at corpus scale — every author named in any lyric, with the line quoted — not a keyword grep.
InputsThe full corpus of songs (or transcripts, or a long text).
OperationsSpawn parallel close-reading subagents, each told to read like a scholar and refuse to grep; aggregate.
OutputsOne aggregated JSON plus a prose writeup pairing each name with its quoted line.
Best fitCodesubagents, scale
?The Gesamtkunstwerk Beyond the map

The term is Wagner's: every medium in a single work. Past the bottom of this axis are combinations of the rows above — text, application, visualization, sound, physical space in one project — that become buildable as the remaining technical barriers come down. Too early to catalogue.

The whole axis is one loop: inputs, operations, outputs. Descent changes a single variable — how much of the loop is code. Worked versions of most rows live in this repo's examples/more-examples/.
