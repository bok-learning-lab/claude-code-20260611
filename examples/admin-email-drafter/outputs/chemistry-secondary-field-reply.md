# Draft reply — Chemistry: secondary field

## Email body

Hi Sofia,

Welcome, and great that chemistry has become part of how you think about your physics work — the
secondary field is a good fit for exactly that. Here are answers to your questions, and we'd be
glad to map out a plan with you whenever you'd like.

1. The secondary field is six letter-graded courses in chemistry, and at least one of them has to
   be an upper-level chemistry course — that means CHEM 40 or any 100- or 200-level chemistry
   course. You need to earn a C- or better in each of the six. (The one exception to the
   letter-grade rule is a designated First-Year Seminar, which is graded SAT/UNS.)

2. Yes and no, in a helpful way. A term of research for credit — CHEM 91R, 98R, or 99R — may count
   toward the secondary field, so your CHEM 91R from last spring can count toward the six. What it
   cannot do is serve as your upper-level course; the research courses don't satisfy that part. So
   you'd still want one upper-level chemistry course (CHEM 40 or a 100/200-level course) among your
   six.

3. Possibly, yes. One course taken abroad may count toward a secondary field in Chemistry if it's
   approved by petition to the Director of Undergraduate Studies. It isn't automatic and depends on
   the specific course, so once you know what you might take abroad, send us the details and we can
   review it with you.

The best first step is simply to let us know you intend to pursue the secondary field — we'll add
you to the Chemistry email list, and you're warmly welcome at our undergraduate events. Email us at
chemistryDUS@fas.harvard.edu and we can sit down with your six-course plan.

Best,
Dr. John Smith
Assistant Director of Undergraduate Studies, Chemistry
Science Center 114
