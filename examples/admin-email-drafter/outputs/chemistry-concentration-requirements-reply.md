## Email body

Hi Priya,

Glad the first round helped — and good timing, because I can now close the loop on the pass/fail number I owed you. Answers to both questions below.

On pass/fail: the cap is two. Two courses counted for concentration credit may be taken pass/fail. And yes, what you heard is right — the research tutorials (CHEM 91R, 98R, and 99R) are graded SAT/UNS, and those grades do not count against the two-course pass/fail limit. So if you do research for credit down the line, it won't use up either of your pass/fail slots.

On related fields: they count toward the concentration, not on top of it. "Related fields" here means courses in Physics and Mathematics (including Applied Physics and Applied Math), plus upper-level Biology, Biochemistry, and Earth and Planetary Sciences courses that carry a chemistry prerequisite. The math and physics you're already planning are in fact required pieces of the concentration — at least one math course (MATH 21A or equivalent, with 21B strongly recommended) and at least two physics courses — and they sit inside the total, not beyond it.

As for the overall number: the basic concentration is 12 to 14 courses (48–56 credits), of which at least eight must be in chemistry. The exact figure depends on your math placement — 12 if you start in MATH 21A or beyond, 13 if you place into MATH 1B, and 14 if you need to take MATH 1A. Whatever room is left after the required chemistry, math, and physics can be filled with additional chemistry or related-field courses to reach that total.

That should be everything you need to finish the plan of study, but Dr. Tucci or I are happy to look it over with you once you've sketched it out.
