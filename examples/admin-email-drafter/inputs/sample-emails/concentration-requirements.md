Hi Priya,

Great that you're planning ahead, and happy to help untangle this. Quick answers below; Dr. Tucci or Dr. Smith can go deeper at your advising meeting.

On general chemistry: yes, LS 1A can satisfy that piece, but the general chemistry requirement is satisfied either by a single course (Chem 10) OR by two courses — one being LS 1A (or LPS A) and the other being Physical Sciences 10 or 11. So with LS 1A you'd still want to pair it with PHYSCI 10 or 11. You would not also need Chem 10.

On the track: both the 17/27 and the 20/30 sequences count equally for the concentration, so 17/27 is completely fine. The difference is emphasis, not legitimacy.

On pass/fail: I'll confirm the exact number in a follow-up, but there is a cap and the research tutorial grades are treated separately.
