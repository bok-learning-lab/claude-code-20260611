Hi Marcus,

You have not missed the boat at all — junior year is exactly the right time to start, so this is good timing.

A couple of things to know up front. The thesis in Chemistry is optional: it is not required for the degree, and it is tied to the honors track rather than the basic track. The mechanism is the research tutorial sequence: many students start research as a junior in CHEM 98R, continue it senior year in CHEM 99R, and students enrolled in CHEM 99R have the option of writing a thesis. So the practical path is: find a research group, enroll in the tutorial, and the thesis grows out of that work.

One administrative detail people often miss: before you can enroll in CHEM 98R (or 91R/99R), you have to register the name of your research mentor with our office. So securing a lab comes first.

Dr. Tucci and I are glad to talk it through. Let me know what other questions you have.
