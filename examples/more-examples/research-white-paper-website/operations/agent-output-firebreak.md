# Operation — The agent-output firebreak

*A structural convention rather than a piece of code: the research repo dedicates **one top-level section** (`agent-output/`) to AI-generated artifacts, kept structurally distinct from human-authored research. A reader navigating the site can see at a glance which parts of the research are the human's own work and which are AI-assisted. The firebreak is in the file tree.*

---

## The convention

The five sections declared in [`lib-content.ts`](lib-content.ts) are:

| Section | Authorship |
|---|---|
| `sources/` | Other people's published work — primary texts (Old English Herbarium translation, Review of Scholarship). Not the researcher's authorship at all; cited material. |
| `analysis/` | The researcher's own analytical work — candidate remedies, expanded candidates, deep research findings, theoretical framework, paper arcs. |
| `drafts/` | The researcher's own paper drafts — paper1 (ethnopharmacology), paper2 (intellectual history), paper3 (folklore). |
| `proposal/` | The researcher's own experimental proposal. |
| **`agent-output/`** | **AI-generated drafts, syntheses, and bibliographies produced during the research process.** |

The firebreak is `agent-output/` vs. everything else under `analysis/`, `drafts/`, and `proposal/`. The first is *AI did this; the researcher reviewed and decided whether to use it*. The rest is *the researcher wrote this themselves*.

The site's landing page surfaces all five sections as equal navigable cards, with the `agent-output` card's blurb stating its authorship explicitly: *"Drafts, syntheses, and bibliographies generated by AI agents."* No euphemism, no "AI-assisted." The reader sees, on the landing page, that there is a separate section for AI work.

## Why this is one of the load-bearing moves in the project

Academic research with AI assistance has a *trust problem*: a reader cannot tell, from a polished paper, where the AI helped and where it didn't. The firebreak is one answer — instead of mixing AI-generated drafts into the same files as human-authored analysis (and either crediting them invisibly or scrubbing them), they live in a separate folder that anyone can browse.

This makes the research *auditable*:

- A skeptical reader can click into `agent-output/` and see exactly what the AI was producing during the research process.
- A trusting reader can ignore `agent-output/` entirely and read only the human-authored work in `analysis/`, `drafts/`, and `proposal/`.
- A reader curious about *the research process itself* can compare an AI-generated read-through (`agent-output/read-through-2026-05-05.md`) against the human-authored analysis (`analysis/deep_research_findings.md`) and see what the researcher kept, transformed, and rejected.

The firebreak doesn't *prevent* AI assistance from influencing the human-authored sections — of course the researcher reads the AI's drafts and absorbs ideas. But it makes the AI artifacts available as a separate-but-co-located reference object, so the human authorship of `analysis/` and `drafts/` is *not* hiding AI provenance behind a polished surface.

## The convention's three commitments

1. **Agent-output is a top-level section, not nested inside other sections.** This is the firebreak. Not `analysis/agent-drafts/` (which would mix authorship within a section), not `_agent-output/` (which would obscure it under a hidden-folder convention). Top level, named for what it is.

2. **Files in `agent-output/` are kept in their original form**, with their original timestamps and as-generated metadata. They are *not* polished after the fact. A read-through generated on 2026-05-05 is `agent-output/read-through-2026-05-05.md`, dated in the filename. If the researcher later refines and uses parts of it, the refined version goes in `analysis/` or `drafts/` — it doesn't overwrite the original agent-output. The two coexist.

3. **The label is honest.** The section's blurb says *"generated by AI agents"* — not *"AI-assisted notes"* or *"research aids"* or *"computational supplements."* The plain naming is part of the firebreak's function; soft language defeats the audit.

## What this convention deliberately doesn't do

- **It doesn't claim the human-authored sections are AI-free.** The researcher reads the agent's outputs and is influenced by them; absorbed ideas don't get re-quarantined back into `agent-output/`. The firebreak is about *provenance of the artifacts*, not *provenance of the ideas*. The agent's *outputs* live in `agent-output/`; the researcher's *writing* — even where that writing draws on what the agent produced — lives in `analysis/` or `drafts/`.
- **It doesn't grade or rank the agent outputs.** No "good vs. bad" labeling. The reader judges.
- **It doesn't anonymize the agent.** When a file is generated by a specific model or system, the filename and the file's own header are encouraged to say so (`agent-output/van-arsdall-translator-choices.md` includes its provenance at the top). This is not a privacy concern in research-with-AI; it's a citation requirement.
- **It doesn't restrict what the agent works on.** Agent-output can be primary-source synthesis, bibliography compilation, exploratory analysis, translation comparison, anything. The firebreak is about *where it lives*, not *what it covers*.

## Adapting to other research projects

The convention generalizes:

- **Add an `agent-output/` (or `ai-output/`, `model-output/`, etc.) section** to your research repo at the top level.
- **Put AI-generated artifacts there in their original form**, with dated filenames when the timing matters.
- **Add it to your `SECTIONS` array** (see [`research-site-architecture.md`](research-site-architecture.md)) alongside your existing sections, so it shows up as a navigable card on the landing page.
- **Don't fold AI outputs into the human-authored sections** even if it would be more convenient.
- **State the section's purpose plainly** in its blurb. Don't soft-pedal.

The pattern is the same posture you'll find in other gallery examples that surface AI's limits structurally rather than hide them in a footnote — [`oral-exam-practice-bot`](../../oral-exam-practice-bot/)'s no-grading constitution, [`image-API-widget`](../../image-API-widget/)'s rules-in-UI conceit, [`literature-course-concept-website`](../../literature-course-concept-website/)'s three-failure-modes pedagogical arc, and [`film-course-concepts-website`](../../film-course-concepts-website/)'s "AI as Lab Partner, Not Ghostwriter" framing. The common move: **make AI's role in the work structurally legible in the artifact itself**, not hidden behind a privacy policy or a methodology note.

## Hard constraints (these survive translation)

- **Top-level section, not nested.** The firebreak only works if the section is visible from the landing page.
- **Original form, not polished.** Agent outputs are not edited after the fact; if you want to use them, take the parts you want into a new file in `analysis/` or `drafts/` and leave the original intact.
- **Honest label.** The section's blurb names the authorship plainly. No euphemisms.
- **Co-located, not segregated.** The firebreak's value comes from `agent-output/` being navigable next to `analysis/` and `drafts/` — same site, same navigation. Hiding it on a different domain or behind a login defeats the audit purpose.
- **The convention is descriptive, not prescriptive.** It doesn't require AI to be used in any particular way; it requires that if AI is used, the outputs are visible.
