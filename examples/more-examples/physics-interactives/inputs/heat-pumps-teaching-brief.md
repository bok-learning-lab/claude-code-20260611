# Heat pumps — a faculty teaching brief

A sample input the `/phet-sim` and `/phet-activity` skills can be exercised against. Captures a real faculty teaching goal in the form the skills expect: a learning goal, a target audience, a content scope, a source text, and constraints on rigor and supporting materials.

## The teaching goal

Teach STEM students in the second semester of the first year of Harvard College how heat pumps work.

## The target learner

Students have already learned math at the level of Math 1B and are concurrently taking Math 21A.

## The content scope

Present a lecture to introduce them to the important thermodynamics concepts of entropy and Carnot cycle and Carnot efficiency. And why electrical power from solar cells is so efficient for moving a car and driving a heat pump compared to the heat energy from burning fossil fuels.

And show them how to calculate the efficiency of heat pumps.

## The source text

Base it on the thermodynamics teachings in the book by David Goodstein: *States of Matter*.

This is an excellent and high-quality book on thermodynamics and statistical mechanics but it is way too high level for first-year students.

## The rigor constraint

The arguments we need to come up with need to be for these second-semester students but the arguments have to be solid and not fluffy.

## Supporting materials requested

Include useful illustrations. Also generate supporting quizzes and homework prompts.
