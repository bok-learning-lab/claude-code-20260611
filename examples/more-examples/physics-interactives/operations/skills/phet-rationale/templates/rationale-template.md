## The design choices in this artifact

[Walk through the specific decisions in *this* sim and map each to a research-validated PhET principle. Use the simulation's header design record as the source for the choices. Use bullet form if it helps clarity. Examples of the form:]

- **What is shown, what is hidden:** [The simulation exposes X and Y; it hides Z. The hidden variables are the pedagogical move — they preserve the structural relationship that matters while removing detail that would obscure it (Bruner, 1960; Adams et al., 2008b).]
- **Linked representations:** [The physical model, the graph, and the readout all update together as the student moves the slider. Multiple linked representations distribute cognitive load and constrain interpretation (Ainsworth, 2006).]
- **Immediate, reversible feedback:** [Every control updates the model in real time, with no submit button. This is what makes the artifact a probe rather than a demonstration.]
- **Reflection prompts that ask for prediction first:** [Built into the artifact as visible "Try this" prompts. The Predict-Observe-Explain sequence is grounded in Hake (1998) and Mazur's Peer Instruction work.]
