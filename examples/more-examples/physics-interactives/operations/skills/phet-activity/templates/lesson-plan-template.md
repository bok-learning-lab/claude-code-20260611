## In-class activity

**Total time:** [N minutes]

### Phase 1 — Predict (commitment) | [X minutes]

[A specific scenario; students individually write their prediction *with reasoning* on paper or in a shared doc. No sim access yet. The prediction must target the misconception, not a generic question.]

**Student-facing prompt** (exact wording for handout or board):

> [Exact wording.]

**Instructor moves during this phase:**

- Walk the room. Do not answer the prompt.
- If a student finishes early, ask them to write *why* they predicted that.
- If a student says "I don't know," ask them to predict anyway — the act of committing is the active-learning trigger, even if the prediction is wrong.

### Phase 2 — Observe (sim exploration) | [X minutes]

[Pair or small-group structure. Students open the sim and work through specific tasks. Each task targets one aspect of the misconception. Do not tell them what to look for in advance — the prompts ask them to *do*, then *notice*.]

**Tasks (in order):**

1. [Task 1 with specific parameter settings, e.g. "Set m = 1, k = 10, c = 1, x₀ = 1. Press Release. Record how long it takes for the oscillation amplitude to drop to half its starting value."]
2. [Task 2 — varies one parameter and predicts before observing.]
3. [Task 3 — sets up the misconception confrontation directly.]

**Instructor moves during this phase:**

- Listen for students who are *not* surprised — they may have prior conceptual knowledge, or they may not have committed to a real prediction. Both warrant a follow-up question.
- Resist the urge to explain. Hold the explanation until Phase 4.

### Phase 3 — Explain (peer discussion) | [X minutes]

[Think-pair-share, or pair-only at minimum. Full-class share follows.]

**Prompts for pair discussion:**

> Prompt 1: [Compare your prediction to what you observed. Where did they disagree? Where did they agree? Why?]
>
> Prompt 2: [In your own words, what rule is the simulation following? Write a single sentence you would defend to the instructor.]

**Full-class share-out:**

[Solicit two or three pair responses. Do not validate them yet. Note any disagreements on the board.]

### Phase 4 — Synthesize (instructor wrap) | [X minutes]

[The instructor names the misconception, names the resolution, and connects to what students just observed.]

**Key points to make (in this order):**

1. [Restate the misconception aloud. Tell students this is a *common* belief — normalizing it makes students more willing to admit they held it.]
2. [State the actual rule, ideally tied to specific observations students just made in the sim.]
3. [Name the limits of the model — what the sim does *not* show that would matter in real life. This is where the sim's "Model limitations" panel pays off.]

## Instructor facilitation notes

### Discussion management

- **If discussion stalls:** [Specific prompt to break silence, usually a forced-choice question — "Raise your hand if your prediction matched. Raise your hand if it didn't."]

## Self-score (lesson-quality checklist)

Run through this before delivering the lesson. Non-negotiable items must pass; strongly-recommended items may be waived only with a reason.

**Non-negotiable:**

- [ ] P-O-E-S structure present: Predict → Observe → Explain → Synthesize, in order.
- [ ] Commitment moment: students predict in writing *before* exploring the sim.
- [ ] Misconception-anchored prediction (not a generic question).
- [ ] Behavioral learning objectives (no "understand" or "appreciate").
- [ ] At least two expected wrong predictions named with prepared instructor responses.
- [ ] Every in-class phase has an explicit minute budget.

**Strongly recommended (waive only with reason):**

- [ ] Peer-discussion phase (think-pair-share or pair-only). *Waive only if:* [reason, e.g. fully asynchronous remote].
- [ ] Pre-class prep with prior-belief surfacing. *Waive only if:* [reason].
- [ ] Follow-up assessment distinguishing conceptual understanding from rote application. *Waive only if:* [reason, e.g. assessment lives elsewhere].
- [ ] Tech setup checklist concrete enough that a TA could run the lesson cold.
- [ ] Total phase time matches the user's stated time budget.

**Good to have:**

- [ ] Backup plan for tech failure.
- [ ] Discussion contingencies (stall, off-topic, dominate, premature agreement).
- [ ] Modality-specific facilitation moves.
- [ ] Connection to the rest of the course (what precedes, what follows).
