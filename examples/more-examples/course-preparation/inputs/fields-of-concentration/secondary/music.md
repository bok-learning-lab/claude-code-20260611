# Music

The Secondary Field in Music equips students with a practical and critical understanding of
music by exploring a wide variety of musical styles, genres, and traditions. A wide range of
courses are available in music theory, composition, historical musicology, and
ethnomusicology, as well as creatively focused courses that offer opportunities for music
performance, composition, and improvisation. These courses reflect the specialties of our
academic faculty; classical, opera, jazz, music and politics, early music, musical theater,
music and media, improvisation, hip-hop, music in film, American and European modernism,
music and ecology, music and language, new music of the 21st century, and the musical
traditions of Africa, Latin America, and Asia.

Department of Music courses provide an academic focus that supports the intellectual
development of musicians, while also incorporating creative opportunities for music
performance, composition, and improvisation. Students are free to explore the field by
selecting a variety of courses that best reflect their interests and aptitudes.

REQUIREMENTS

Music: 5 courses (20 credits)

Any five courses selected from among the courses offered in Music (including General
Education courses and First-Year Seminars), with the requirements noted below:

   1. At least one course is required from each of the three course categories (12 credits):
          a. History, Culture, Society (4 credits)
          b. Music Theory (4 credits)
          c. Creating Music (4 credits)
   2. Electives: Two courses (8 credits total):
          a. All Department of Music courses satisfy concentration elective requirements. In
              addition:
                  i.  One First-Year Seminar taught by Department of Music faculty can
                      count.
                 ii.  Gen Ed courses taught by a Department of Music faculty member can
                      count.
                iii.  Multiple introductory-level courses will be eligible only by approval from
                      the DUS.
   3. Other Information:
          a. Students participating in faculty-led large ensembles (Harvard Radcliffe
              Orchestra, Harvard-Radcliffe Collegium, Harvard Glee Club, Radcliffe Choral
              Society, Harvard Jazz Orchestra) may take these courses for secondary credit
              in the following ways:
                  i.  Music 110, 114, 115, 116, or 118 must be taken twice (4 credits total) to
                      count toward a single Category or Elective requirement.
                 ii.  These courses cannot be counted more than twice toward secondary
                      credit.
          b. Music 180 New Music Ensemble, Music 186 Contemporary Chamber Music,

              and Music 189 Chamber Music Performance cannot be counted more than
              twice (8 credits total) toward secondary credit.
         c. Courses counting for Secondary credit may not normally be taken pass/fail,
              other than one First-Year Seminar (graded SAT/UNSAT).
   4. Courses taken outside the Department of Music will be considered for secondary
      credit at the discretion of the DUS.
         a. Courses proposed for secondary credit must not compete with course offerings
              available in the Harvard Department of Music. Students must schedule an
              advising session with the DUS to confirm course eligibility prior to registration
              or transferring.
         b. Courses proposed from outside the Department of Music must be taken for a
              minimum of 4 credits each. Courses taken for fewer than 4 credits will be
              considered on a case-by-case basis, at the discretion of the DUS. Courses
              taken for 1 credit will not be considered.
   5. Students participating in the joint studies program at Berklee College of Music or the
      dual degree program at the New England Conservatory of Music may transfer a
      maximum of 3 courses to Harvard for academic credit, with a maximum of 2 courses
      counting towards a secondary in Music.
         a. Courses proposed for transfer must not compete with course offerings
              available in the Harvard Department of Music and are approved at the
              discretion of the Department of Music DUS. Students should meet with the
              DUS to confirm course eligibility prior to petitioning for transfer credit.
         b. Private instruction and/or one-on-one instrumental, vocal, or composition
              lessons are not eligible for transfer credit.
         c. Students may transfer a maximum of 2 courses per semester.
         d. One-credit courses are not eligible for transfer. Two-, three-, and four-credit
              courses are eligible for transfer credit, at the discretion of the Department of
              Music DUS.
         e. Participation in Harvard’s joint programs of study with Berklee and NEC cannot
              be used as a mechanism to reduce the undergraduate course load at Harvard.
              Students are expected to meet the requirements for undergraduate progress.
              Details on the required rate of progress are included in the Harvard Student
              Handbook.
         f. To receive Harvard credit students must complete and submit the Proposal
              Form for Dual Programs of Study out of Residence. More information is
              available on the Dual Programs of Study website
              https://music.fas.harvard.edu/academics/undergraduate-study-in-
              musicinformation-for-prospective-students/about-the-dual-programs-of-study/.

OTHER INFORMATION

The Department of Music welcomes all students with an interest in pursuing musical studies.
For further information, students should contact the Director of Undergraduate (DUS) or the
Undergraduate Program Coordinator (UPC) by scheduling an appointment using the
Department of Music website at https://music.fas.harvard.edu/academics/undergraduate-
study-in-music-information-for-current-students.

ADVISING RESOURCES AND EXPECTATIONS

Advising in the Department of Music is carried out by the DUS with support from the UPC.
The DUS has overall responsibility for the academic progress of undergraduates and is
available by appointment to review programs of study. Students interested in declaring a
Secondary in Music are required to schedule an advising appointment with either the DUS or
the UPC prior to completing a Secondary Field request in my.harvard. Students should work
with advising resources to develop a strategy for fulfillment of academic requirements.

More information on undergraduate advising in the Department of Music can be found by
visiting the Department of Music website at
https://music.fas.harvard.edu/academics/undergraduate-study-in-music-information-for-
current-students. Students may schedule advising sessions with either DUS or the UPC.
