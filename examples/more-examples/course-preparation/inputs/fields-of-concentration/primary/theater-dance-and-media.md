# Theater, Dance & Media

Theater, Dance & Media (TDM) combines historical and theoretical study of live and digital
arts with the practice of those arts. Taught by Faculty of Arts and Sciences (FAS) faculty, and
by practitioners from the American Repertory Theater (A.R.T.) and numerous visiting
artists/scholars each year, students are introduced to the rigor and discipline required to
participate in professional theater, dance, and media practices. The goal of the concentration
is to foster the imagination and intellect using a broad range of arts-based research and
practice techniques that offer strong training in creating expressive culture. Students are
strongly encouraged to work across art forms, including literature, music, and the visual arts,
and beyond the humanities, in order to incorporate new ideas and forms of knowledge into
their work in the performing arts.
TDM emphasizes collaboration. The concentration explores the many ways to act, design,
direct, compose, choreograph, dance, write, produce, and organize live art and digital media.
Our studio courses model and study the many forms of arts collaboration; similarly, scholarly
courses look to the history and theory of culture as a complement to the skills gained in studio
practice.
In addition to the required course work, students pursuing the Theater, Dance & Media
concentration are required to meaningfully participate in at least one TDM concentration
production studio course led by professional directors, choreographers, and designers.
Concentrators will also obtain valuable technical skills through both working on the crew for at
least one concentration show and supporting the work of their peers.
LEARNING OBJECTIVES
The goal of the concentration is to foster the imagination and intellect using a broad range of arts-
based research and practice techniques. TDM offers training in a variety of methods to create
expressive culture. Students are strongly encouraged to work across art forms—including
literature, music, and the visual arts as well as with and beyond the other disciplines in the
humanities—to incorporate new ideas and forms of knowledge into their work in the performing
arts.
REQUIREMENTS
Theater, Dance & Media
Basic Program Requirements: 12 courses (48 credits)
1. Required Courses:
a. Three courses focused on critical and scholarly approaches to theater, dance,
and media.
b. Three courses that are practice-based or studio.
c. Two elective courses, one of which may be in a related field (with approval
from the Director of Undergraduate Studies).
d. One course in design or technical aspects of performance.
e. One TDM production studio.
f. Note: At least one of the courses in (a), (b), (c), or (d) must focus on non-
Western or non-traditional theatrical practices. Additionally, at least one of
those courses must focus on pre-20th-century performance. Finally, at least
one of those courses must focus on theater, one on dance, and one on media.
2. Tutorials:
a. Sophomore year: TDM 97: Sophomore Tutorial: Foundational Concepts in
Theater, Dance & Media. One term. Required. Letter-graded. Ordinarily taken

in the spring of sophomore year.
b. Junior year: TDM 98: Junior Tutorial. One term. Required. Letter-graded.
Ordinarily taken in the fall of junior year.
3. Thesis: None.
4. General examination: None.
5. Other information:
a. Grading basis: Ordinarily, courses counting for concentration credit may not be
taken pass/fail or SAT/UNS; however, one First-Year Seminar may be counted
for concentration credit if taught by a TDM faculty member and/or consistent
with TDM concentration offerings,
b. The following additional requirements will be graded SAT/UNS and must be
approved by the Director of Undergraduate Studies.
i. Crew Assignment: Students must complete one production crew
assignment by working on one TDM production or TDM thesis
production.
ii. One mandatory workshop on theater safety completed concurrently
with enrollment in TDM 97.
iii. Concentration grade-point average: All letter-graded courses that can
count for the TDM concentration will count for the concentration grade-
point average.
c. Concentration grade-point average: All letter-graded courses that can count for
the TDM concentration will count for the concentration grade-point average.
Theater, Dance & Media
Honors Eligibility Requirements: 14 courses (56 credits)
1. Required courses: Same as Basic Requirements.
2. Tutorials: Same as Basic Requirements, plus TDM 99A and TDM 99B: Senior
Tutorial. Graded SAT/UNS.
3. Thesis: Required. Honors candidates may complete a research paper or production
thesis. Thesis ideas are proposed in the second semester of the junior year and must
be approved by the Executive Committee on Theater, Dance & Media, and the DUS.
Honors candidates must have completed prior coursework that supports their thesis
(e.g., a directing thesis student must have taken at least one directing course, etc.).
4. General examination: None.
5. Other information: Same as Basic Requirements.

For information on joint concentrations, students should consult the Assistant Director of

ADVISING
The Assistant Director of Undergraduate Studies (ADUS) advises all students when they
enter the concentration, eventually connecting them with additional faculty and professional
mentors, based on the students’ particular interests and their work in or on specific
productions. Together with the ADUS, these advisors will support students in developing a
coherent course of study and a solid body of artistic work that is suited to each student’s goals
and interests.
For up-to-date information on advising in Theater, Dance & Media, students should see the
Advising Programs Office website at https://advising.college.harvard.edu/concentrations.
HOW TO FIND OUT MORE
For more information about the concentration, students should visit the department website at
https://tdm.fas.harvard.edu/ or email tdmdus@fas.harvard.edu.
