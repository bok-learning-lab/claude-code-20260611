What if a high entropy system in one context represented a very ordered and low entropy system in another, with different physics rules? This is the concept of “relative entropy.” It suggests that the measure of disorder in a system is not absolute, but relative to the frame of reference or set of physical rules that define it. Take the example of a gas observed under classical mechanics, appearing highly disordered and chaotic, yet appearing highly ordered and structured when observed under quantum mechanics.
