# R003 — Carla

## INDEX: background-education (lines 12–18 in R003-Transcript.md)

**I:** Thanks for squeezing this in before work. Can we start with your own school experience growing up?

**CARLA:** Sure. So I'm forty-four, grew up here, went to public schools. I was a B student, B-plus when I tried. Mostly I just wanted to get through. After high school I went to the community college and got my associate's in medical billing. That was — okay, that's actually relevant. I didn't really want to go to community college. I wanted to go to a four-year. My guidance counselor was the one who said community college, get a credential, get into the workforce. And — she wasn't wrong, exactly. I make decent money at the clinic. But I always wondered.

**I:** Wondered what?

**CARLA:** What it would have been like. To go away. To take classes that weren't just — medical billing. I took one literature class as an elective and I remember thinking, oh, *this* is what college is. This is what people are talking about. And then I never got to take another one because I was on the medical billing track. So.

## INDEX: hopes-after-hs (lines 28–38 in R003-Transcript.md)

**I:** How is it a problem?

**CARLA:** Because he doesn't know what he wants. And I — I go back and forth with him about whether that matters.

**I:** Tell me more about that.

**CARLA:** So like — half the time I'm telling him, Marcus, you don't need to have it figured out, you can go to college and try things, take a philosophy class, take an art class, see what speaks to you. That's what I would have wanted. That's the version of me that didn't get to do it talking. And then the other half of the time I'm telling him, look, Marcus, you need to think about what you can actually do with this, you need to think about how we're going to pay for it, you need to look at the data on what majors lead to jobs. And he looks at me like — Mom, which is it? Which Carla am I talking to today? And honestly I don't know.

**I:** That's really honest. Can you say more about the two voices?

**CARLA:** [pause] Yeah. So the first voice, the "go explore" voice, that's — I think that's what I believe in my heart. I think college should be that. I think being twenty should be that. I read these articles about how college has become this credentialing factory and it makes me angry. Like, that's not what it was supposed to be. But then I look at our bank account and I look at the price of the schools and I look at Marcus's friends whose parents have lawyers and consultants and I think — we cannot afford to be romantic about this. He has to come out the other side with a job. Because there's no safety net. If he comes out with eighty thousand in debt and no job, that ruins his life. That ruins my life. So the practical voice is the one that — that voice is paying attention to the actual world we're in.

## INDEX: hopes-after-hs (lines 52–58 in R003-Transcript.md)

**I:** Has Marcus said what *he* wants?

**CARLA:** He says he wants to "do something meaningful." Which is the most seventeen-year-old answer of all time. I love him for it. But I also think — okay, meaningful, sure, but it also has to pay. And he hears me say both of those things and I think he's confused. I'm confused. **CARLA:** Class, probably. [laughs] I mean — really. If we had money, the "go explore" voice would just win. There would be no conflict. The conflict is because we don't have money. If somebody gave us a hundred thousand dollars tomorrow I would tell Marcus to study English without blinking.

## INDEX: worries-barriers (lines 60–62 in R003-Transcript.md)

**I:** What worries you most?

**CARLA:** That he'll make the choice for the wrong reasons. Either way. That he'll do engineering because he thinks he's supposed to and be miserable. Or he'll do English because he thinks he's supposed to and be broke. I don't want him picking based on what he thinks I want. I want him picking based on what he wants. But I haven't given him a clear signal about what *I* want, because I don't know what I want. So.
