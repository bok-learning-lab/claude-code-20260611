# Methods (project state as of 2026-05-21)

This Methods write-up describes the project as it currently stands. Analytic steps that have not yet been performed are noted explicitly rather than glossed.

## Data

The corpus comprises N=4 transcripts of semistructured interviews with low-income mothers of teenagers about college planning for their children: [R001 (Tasha)](inputs/transcripts/R001-Transcript.md), [R002 (Marisol)](inputs/transcripts/R002-Transcript.md), [R003 (Carla)](inputs/transcripts/R003-Transcript.md), and [R004 (Denise)](inputs/transcripts/R004-Transcript.md). The transcripts are **synthetic**, generated for a workshop demonstration of LLM-assisted flexible coding, and were modeled on Deterding's (2015) study "Instrumental and Expressive Education: College Planning in the Face of Poverty" (*Sociology of Education* 88(4)); they do not represent real respondents. Per-respondent attributes are recorded in [inputs/attributes.csv](inputs/attributes.csv) and cover respondent ID, pseudonym, age, race/ethnicity, highest education, employment, household income bracket, marital status, number of children, age of the oldest child, interview date, location, and interviewer. Filenames follow Deterding & Waters' (2018, p. 723) convention of leading the document with the respondent ID so attributes can be auto-linked to documents. Respondents range in age from 36 to 44 and were interviewed across a four-week period in March 2024 by a single interviewer (ND).

## Protocol

Interviews followed a seven-section semistructured guide ([inputs/protocol.md](inputs/protocol.md)): (1) background and the respondent's own education; (2) family and children; (3) hopes and plans after high school; (4) college specifically; (5) sources of advice and information; (6) worries and barriers; (7) closing reflections. Interviews were designed to run 60–90 minutes.
