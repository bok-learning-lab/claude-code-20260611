# Sample interview corpus (synthetic)

**These transcripts are entirely synthetic.** They were generated to support workshop demos of LLM-assisted flexible coding (Deterding & Waters 2018). Any resemblance to real respondents is coincidental.

## Study design (simulated)

Topic: College planning among low-income mothers of teenagers. Modeled on Deterding 2015, "Instrumental and Expressive Education: College Planning in the Face of Poverty" (*Sociology of Education* 88(4)), which is the worked example Deterding & Waters return to throughout their methods paper.

The four transcripts are designed to vary along the *instrumental / expressive / mixed* typology so workshop demos can show typology assignment, construct-validity checks, negative-case hunting, and great-quote surfacing.

## Design choices for demo value

| ID | Pseudonym | Designed type | Demo affordance |
|----|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| R001 | Tasha | Instrumental | Clear, textbook case |
| R002 | Marisol | Expressive | Clear, textbook case |
| R003 | Carla | Mixed | Self-aware tension — good for typology stress test |
| R004 | Denise | Instrumental (emotionally framed) | Surface vocabulary reads expressive; reasoning is instrumental. Good `/construct-validity-check` demo — naive coders may miscode. |

## Conventions

Filenames follow Deterding & Waters' p. 723 advice (`R001-Transcript.md`) so attributes can be auto-linked to documents by leading respondent ID. Speakers marked with `I:` for interviewer and the respondent's pseudonym in ALL CAPS.
