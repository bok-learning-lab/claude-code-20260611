# Interview protocol — College planning among low-income mothers

Semistructured. Approximate duration: 60–90 minutes. Sections are roughly sequential but the interviewer follows the respondent's leads.

## 1. Background and your own education

- Tell me a bit about where you grew up.
- What was school like for you?
- Did you go to college, or think about going? What happened?

## 2. Your family now

- Tell me about your kids.
- How are they doing in school?
- What are they into? What do their teachers say about them?

## 3. Hopes and plans after high school

- Have you started thinking about what your kids will do after high school?
- What do you hope for them?
- If you've talked with them about it, what have those conversations been like?

## 4. College specifically

- When you hear the word "college," what comes to mind?
- What do you think college is *for*?
- Have you looked at specific schools or programs?
- What do you know about how college works — applications, costs, financial aid?

## 5. Sources of advice and information

- Where do you go for advice or information about this?
- Who in your life has been through it?
- What about school counselors, websites, family, neighbors?

## 6. Worries and barriers

- What worries you when you think about your kids and college?
- What feels like it's in the way?

## 7. Closing reflections

- Is there anything you wish was different about how this all works?
- Anything I didn't ask that I should have?
