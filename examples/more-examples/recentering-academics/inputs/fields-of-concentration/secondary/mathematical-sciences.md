# Mathematical Sciences

The secondary field in Mathematical Sciences is jointly sponsored by the Mathematics
Department and the Applied Mathematics concentration.

REQUIREMENTS

Mathematical Sciences: 4 courses (16 credits)

Four courses in either Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, or Statistics, of which at most two
can be in Statistics. The Mathematics and Applied Mathematics courses must be numbered
104 or higher; and Statistics courses must be numbered 110 or higher.

OTHER INFORMATION

Courses must be taken for a letter grade and cannot be taken pass/fail. Only courses with a
grade of C- or above can be counted.

Students who study abroad or take courses within Harvard Summer School can count course
credits toward the secondary field in Mathematical Sciences by petitioning for such course to
be counted as the equivalent to an approved Harvard course.

Note that courses in other departments that are cross-listed in the course catalog under
Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, or Statistics will count toward secondary field.

ADVISING RESOURCES AND EXPECTATIONS

If students have questions about course selection, they should contact the Director of
Undergraduate Studies for Mathematics, or the Applied Math Advising Team.
