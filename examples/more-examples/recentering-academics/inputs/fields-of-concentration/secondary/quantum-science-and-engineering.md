# Quantum Science and Engineering

Quantum science and engineering (QSE) has emerged as a new interdisciplinary field with
the potential to profoundly impact areas such as computing, communications, materials
science, sensing and metrology, and beyond. While its foundations were laid in the early 20th
century with the development of quantum mechanics, recent scientific and engineering
progress in precise coherent control over quantum states, in information theory, and in
algorithms have opened the door to entirely new opportunities. Advancing these new
opportunities further, requires training in both scientific and engineering aspects of quantum.
The secondary field in QSE is structured to help students build the foundations of quantum
science and engineering and at the same time allowing them either a broad exploration of the
field or a deeper dive into one of its subfields.

While the QSE secondary field is ideally suited for students with a background in Physics,
Chemistry, Mathematics, or Engineering, students from other disciplinary backgrounds with
an inherent interest of the field may be well-suited for the secondary with sufficient
preparation. Interested students are encouraged to be in touch with the director of the
secondary field to understand the necessary preparation and to develop a plan of study.

REQUIREMENTS

Quantum Science and Engineering: 5 courses (20 credits)

Core courses: Two required foundational courses.
   1. QSE 100/ES 170: Engineering Quantum Mechanics. Students with sufficient
      preparation (for example, students who have taken the quantum mechanics sequence
      in Physics, 143 A/B) can alternatively take the graduate-level QSE/ES 200: Advanced
      Engineering Quantum Mechanics.
   2. QSE 110/Physics 160: Introduction to Quantum Information Science – Students with
      sufficient preparation can alternatively take the graduate-level Phys 260 / QSE 210
      (either A or B version).

Elective courses: Two courses chosen from the following list. These elective courses drawn
from Physics, SEAS, and CCB introduce core concepts in various subfields of QSE. Students
could identify a subfield of interest and dive deeper or choose to more broadly explore
different subfields. The list of elective courses will be updated as additional relevant courses
are offered.
     1. ES 173: Introduction to Electronic and Photonic Devices
     2. ES 177: Microfabrication Laboratory
     3. Physics 195A: Introduction to Solid State Physics
     4. Physics 195B: Introduction to Quantum Materials and Devices
     5. Physics 181: Statistical Mechanics and Thermodynamics
     6. ES 151: Applied Electromagnetics
     7. Physics 175: Laser Physics and Modern Optical Physics
     8. Chem 160: The Quantum World

Research Course: One semester of supervised reading and research with a QSE core
faculty member to gain direct exposure to research and cutting-edge experiments and/or
theoretical work in the field. List of QSE core faculty can be found at

https://quantum.harvard.edu/core-faculty.
    1. QSE 91R: Supervised Reading and Research

OTHER INFORMATION

All courses counting towards the QSE secondary field must be taken for a letter grade and
students must earn a C– or better in each course. First-year seminars and non-letter graded
courses cannot be counted towards the secondary field. A mathematics background in
calculus and linear algebra at the level of Math 21A and 21B is prerequisite for many of the
courses in this secondary field. Relevant advanced courses not listed in the requirements
above, courses taken by cross-registration at MIT, study abroad courses, or Harvard Summer
School courses may be counted with prior petition and approval. Petitions should be sent to
the director of the secondary field (see below). Students participating in semester-long
research with faculty not in the core QSE faculty list may be counted towards the research
course credit with prior approval. First year seminars may not be used to fulfil secondary field
requirements. Only one course may be double counted towards both the concentration and
secondary field.

ADVISING RESOURCES AND EXPECTATIONS

Students interested in the QSE secondary field are encouraged to get in touch with the
Director of Education, Dr. Nishant Sule (nsule@fas.harvard.edu), as early as possible to
ensure that they have sufficient preparation and to develop a plan of study.
