# Prompt — "Key Takeaways" from a session transcript

_Reconstructed from the Day 1 output ([`day-1-session-1-key-takeaways.md`](../outputs/day-1-session-1-key-takeaways.md)). Reuse it for any session by pointing it at that day's transcript._

---

I missed this session and want to catch up fast. Read the transcript at
`inputs/<DAY>_transcript.md` and distill it into a **"Top 10 Key Takeaways"**
markdown doc. Write the result to
`outputs/<day>-session-<n>-key-takeaways.md`.

Requirements:

- **Title** the doc `# <Day> / Session <n> — Top 10 Key Takeaways`.
- Open with a one-line **italic provenance note**: what it was distilled from
  (link the transcript file), the chunk/time range covered, and who the
  instructors were (MW = Madeleine, MK = Marlon, plus any faculty/guests).
- **Exactly 10 numbered takeaways**, ordered roughly as they came up. Each one:
  - Leads with a **bold one-sentence headline** — the mantra or claim itself.
  - Then 1–3 sentences of explanation grounded in what *actually happened* in
    the room: name the specific demos, analogies, and turns of phrase the
    instructors used (e.g. the limerick CLAUDE.md, the recipe metaphor, the
    Memento/context-window image). Quote memorable lines.
  - Capture the *teaching point*, not just the fact — why it mattered to faculty.
- Follow the list with a short **"Secondary points worth keeping"** section:
  bulleted, for the smaller but useful details that didn't make the top 10.
- Voice: tight, concrete, faculty-facing. Favor the instructors' own language
  over generic AI-explainer prose. No preamble, no "here's your summary" — just
  the doc.
